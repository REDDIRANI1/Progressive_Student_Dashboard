# Progressive Student Dashboard — Hackathon Brief

## What I built
I built a web app that helps students and mentors understand learning progress clearly. Students can see what they have completed, how much time they have spent, and what to do next. Mentors can quickly spot which students may need support.

## Problem I solved
Learning progress is often scattered across lessons, courses, and activity history. This project brings those signals into one simple dashboard so students stay on track and mentors can act faster.

## Tech stack
- Frontend: React, TypeScript, Vite, Tailwind CSS, Recharts
- Backend: FastAPI, SQLAlchemy, JWT authentication
- Database: SQLite for local demo, PostgreSQL supported for deployment

## Main experience
- Students sign in and view their personal learning dashboard.
- Progress is shown per course, with completed lessons and time spent.
- Charts make learning trends easier to understand.
- Course detail pages show lesson status and recent activity.
- Mentors get a separate dashboard to review assigned students and identify who needs attention.

## Why it matters
The goal was not just to store progress, but to make progress understandable. The app turns raw learning activity into useful feedback for both students and mentors.

## Key highlights
- Role-based experience: students get a personal learning dashboard, while mentors get a separate view to monitor assigned students.
- Seeded demo data: the project includes ready-to-use sample data, so reviewers can run and evaluate the app quickly.
